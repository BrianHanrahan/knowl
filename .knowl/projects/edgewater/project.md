# edgewater

## Boat Handover Notes

### Annual Maintenance (Spring & Fall)

#### Spring Commissioning
- **Engine fogging oil**: Burn off the fogging oil that was applied in the fall. Current Hudsons will heat the engine cylinders and do this automatically.
- **Batteries**: Reconnect the batteries (they are typically disconnected for winter storage).
- **Antifreeze flush**: The saltwater washdown system and live well system will have antifreeze in the lines to prevent freezing and cracking over winter. Run those systems until the antifreeze is fully flushed out.

#### Ongoing / Annual
- **Oil change**: Change the motor oil once a year.
- **Water pump impeller**: The lower unit water pump has a rubber impeller that needs to be replaced every couple of years — it's a wear part.
- **Bottom paint**: Reapply every couple of years. The bottom paint is designed to slowly wear away over time.

---

### Hull & Exterior Care

#### Exterior (Sides)
- The blue color on the hull sides is **not gel coat** — it is **Awlgrip** paint covered with a clearcoat.
- **Do not** buff or wax the exterior. Simply wash it and touch up the clearcoat where it gets scuffed.

#### Interior
- Wax the interior hull surfaces once a year by hand. Easiest to do a little bit at a time as the season goes on.

---

### Motor Care

#### Flushing After Use
- **Flush the motor with freshwater after every use**, unless you'll be using it again within a few hours.
- On the side of the outboard there is a hose fitting — attach a hose to the female end, turn on the freshwater, and let it run for a couple of minutes to wash all the salt water out of the internal components.
- At the marina, there are typically hoses attached to the stanchion a little forward of where the boat sits. People are usually fine with borrowing them as long as you put them back.

#### Fuel System Priming
- If the boat has been sitting unused for a couple of weeks, prime the fuel system before starting.
- Looking toward the stern, in the **left compartment at the stern**, there is a primer bulb. Squeeze it two or three times to prime fuel into the system.

---

### Trailer

- **Every time** you launch or pull the boat, rinse the trailer down with freshwater — especially the wheels, brakes, and any parts that were fully submerged. This will significantly extend the life of the trailer.

---

### Anchor

- The anchor is **oversized** for the boat, which means it will grab the bottom instantly.
- The anchor line tends to get scaly/rough each year. In the spring, pull it out, wash it with soap, let it dry in the sun, and then put it back in the forward hold.
- **Be careful** storing the anchor in the forward locker — if it's not situated properly, it will bang against the hull exterior. Over time this could wear through and create a hole in the hull.

---

### Portable Toilet

- Located inside the console. Very simple and straightforward to use.
- When empty, put some bleach in it.
- Dump it into a regular toilet once there's some waste in it.
- If you're **3+ miles offshore**, you can dump it overboard.
